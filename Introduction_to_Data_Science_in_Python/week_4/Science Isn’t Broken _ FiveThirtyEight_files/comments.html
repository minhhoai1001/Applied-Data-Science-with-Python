Yeah, the article says "Which political party is best for the economy seems like a pretty straightforward question." But from a science point of view, it might not be a good question at all. The p-hacking exercise gets a little way at this, becasue the real problem with the question is that it is actually kind of a vague, undefined problem. For one thing, a politcal party is not a constant value. The values, coalitions, and platforms they represent change over time--for instance, there was a major shift of allegiance during the civil rights period in the 1960s. Eisenhower could be a Democrat today. A lot of questions in politics and economics are very poorly defined this way.
